# Aurelith — World Canon (Launch Scope)

## 1. WORLD CANON (GLOBAL)

**World Name:** Aurelith

**Core Concept:**
Aurelith was once a unified world governed by a force called the Continuum, which regulated time, matter, memory, and life. When the Continuum fractured in a catastrophic event known as The Fracture, reality itself splintered.

The world did not end — it changed.

Civilization collapsed, history fragmented, and survival reshaped belief. From the ruins emerged tribes, each representing a different response to the broken world.

The current era is after the collapse, when truth is uncertain and power is recovered through fragments of the past.

---

## 2. THE CONTINUUM (ABSTRACT — NOT FULLY EXPLAINED)

**Rules:**
- Treated as a mythic–scientific force, not magic
- Its full nature is unknown and intentionally ambiguous
- Different tribes interpret it differently
- The app should NEVER fully explain it at launch

**Usage:**
- Referenced in lore
- Referenced in item descriptions
- Referenced in timeline fragments
- Never exposed as a mechanic yet

---

## 3. THE FRACTURE (FOUNDATIONAL EVENT)

**Definition:**
The Fracture is the single global catastrophe that shattered the Continuum and broke reality.

**Known effects:**
- Cities collapsed
- Time and memory became unreliable
- Some people mutated
- Others bonded with relics, technology, or unseen forces
- Large portions of history were lost or corrupted

**Rules:**
- No single tribe owns the "true" version of the Fracture
- Contradictory accounts are intentional
- The exact cause is unknown at launch

---

## 4. TRIBES (EXISTENCE ONLY — NO FULL DETAILS YET)

**At launch:**
- Tribes exist
- Tribes are not fully explained
- Tribe names and identities are revealed later via cards and content

**Rules:**
- Onboarding and world text should only imply that tribes rose from the ruins
- No tribe is framed as "good" or "evil"
- No central authority exists in the world

---

## 5. PLAYER ROLE (CRITICAL FOR IMMERSION)

**The player is:**
- Not a hero
- Not a ruler
- Not a chosen one

**The player is a Collector of Fragments:**
- Summons characters
- Recovers relics
- Unlocks timeline fragments
- Gradually uncovers conflicting versions of history

The player's progression represents understanding, not dominance.

---

## 6. LORE DELIVERY RULES

**Lore must be:**
- Fragmented
- Optional
- Discoverable
- Never dumped all at once

**Approved delivery methods at launch:**
- Onboarding (abstract only)
- Universe page (1 short paragraph)
- Card flavor text
- Timeline fragments (locked for later, but scaffold-ready)

**Disallowed at launch:**
- Long lore pages
- Full historical timelines
- Tribe encyclopedias

---

## 7. TONE & STYLE GUIDELINES

**Tone:**
- Mythic
- Post-collapse
- Reflective
- Slightly ominous, not dark fantasy

**Style:**
- Short sentences
- Poetic, not academic
- Imply more than explain

**Never:**
- Use modern slang
- Over-explain systems
- Break immersion with meta language

---

## 8. IMPLEMENTATION NOTES

**Ensure:**
- All world references align with the above canon
- No future lore is hardcoded in a way that can't evolve
- The world feels "old, broken, and alive"
- This canon is treated as launch-era truth, not final truth

Future seasons may reinterpret or contradict parts of this world.
